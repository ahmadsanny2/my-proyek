Frequently Asked Question?

Mental health is a state in which a person can realize his or her own abilities, cope with the stresses of everyday life, work productively, and contribute to his or her community. It encompasses emotional, psychological, and social well-being.

Signs of mental health problems can vary, but some common ones include: extreme mood swings, social withdrawal, difficulty concentrating, changes in sleeping or eating patterns, feeling tired all the time, loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities, and thoughts of harming yourself or others.

The causes of mental health problems are complex and can involve a combination of genetic, biological, psychological, and social factors. Some contributing factors include: a family history of mental illness, traumatic events, chronic stress, chemical imbalances in the brain, and certain medical conditions.

How to deal with mental health problems depends on the type and severity of the disorder experienced. Some common approaches include:
  • Therapy: Talk therapy with a psychologist or psychiatrist can help individuals understand and cope with their problems.
  • Medication: Certain medications can help manage the symptoms of certain mental disorders.
  • Social support: Getting support from family, friends, or a support group can be very helpful.
  • Lifestyle changes: Adopting a healthy lifestyle, such as eating nutritious foods, exercising regularly, and getting enough sleep, can also help improve mental health.

Seeking help for mental health issues is just as important as seeking help for physical issues. Untreated mental health issues can lead to serious complications, such as difficulty in relationships, problems with employment, and even the risk of suicide. With proper treatment, many people with mental illness can recover fully and live better lives.
